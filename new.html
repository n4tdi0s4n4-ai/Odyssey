Odysseus

Background:

The story of Odysseus is a Greek epic poem by Homer that recounts the hero's 10-year struggle to return to his home in Ithaca after the Trojan War. Known for his cunning and resourcefulness, Odysseus battled monsters, angered gods, and faced various trials, all while his wife Penelope and son Telemachus fended off suitors in Ithaca. The epic culminates with Odysseus defeating the suitors and reclaiming his throne.

Penelope

Background:

A character in Homer's Odyssey. She was the queen of Ithaca and was the daughter of Spartan king Icarius and Asterodia. The mythological Penelope is known for her fidelity to her husband Odysseus, despite the attention of more than a hundred suitors during his absence. In one source, Penelope's original name was Arnacia or Arnaea.

Telemachus

Background:

Telemachus is the son of Odysseus and Penelope in Greek mythology, a central character in Homer's epic poem, The Odyssey. His story focuses on his journey to find his father after a long absence and his growth into a heroic young man, eventually helping Odysseus defeat the suitors in Ithaca. He is guided by the goddess Athena, often disguised as Mentor, and the first four books of The Odyssey are dedicated to his quest.
